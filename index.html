Jake Lynch tweets in response to the 7 October massacre

Jake Lynch is an Associate Professor in Sociology and Criminology at the University of Sydney, where his webpage describes him as a pioneer in Peace Journalism, active in peace foundations and peace research, and as a winner of the 2017 Luxembourg Peace Prize.

Jake Lynch has a Twitter (X) account. The account was quiet in the months preceding the horrifying 7 October Hamas Massacres. But on the day of the massacres, the account burst into life. Prof Lynch did not post any messages of empathy about the victims. Instead, he retweeted a series of tweets justifying the massacres as `fighting back' , `struggle against occupation' or 'resistance', comparing them to Ukraine's war against Russia's invasion, or quoting Muhammed Deif (the architect of the massacres) approvingly. These posts are all within 24 hours of the massacres, so there is no question that they refer to the Hamas massacres of 7 October.
